A Pomato a day keeps laziness at bay

What is Pomato Timer?

Pomato Timer is a custom single-page Pomodoro timer webapp created with HTML/CSS and vanilla Javascript. This app is a productivity tool that will help you get work done by organizing your time into chunks for both working and breaks. This app was inspired by the Pomodoro technique created by Francesco Cirillo in the 1980s.

The Pomodoro technique

The Pomodoro Technique was developed in the late 1980s by then university student Francesco Cirillo. Cirillo was struggling to focus on his studies and complete assignments. Feeling overwhelmed, he asked himself to commit to just 10 minutes of focused study time. Encouraged by the challenge, he found a tomato (pomodoro in Italian) shaped kitchen timer, and the Pomodoro technique was born.

The technique is simple, find a task to complete and set aside 25 minutes to concentrate on this task. After 25 minutes, you may take a 5 minute break. Count this 25 minute work / 5 minute break as one Pomodoro. After 4 Pomodoros, you may take a 15-30 minute break.

Features

  • Click the "Customize" button to (although not technically Pomodoro) the time duration of the work, short break and long break sessions.
  • Click the "Your Stats" button to track your work and break session. From the bar graph you can see a visual representation of the time spent working and on break. (Each bar graph is permanently set to 6 hours each. Will add feature to change the time goal)
  • Clicking the "Reset" button will reset the time of the current session (either work, short break or long break). Clicking "Reset" will not impact your stats or reset all session time durations back to the Pomodoro technique standard.
